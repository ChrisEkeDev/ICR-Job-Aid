Getting Started

The first step every day will require you to access our Virtual Desktop Interface (VDI), where all of your job duties will be performed. You should have already received your employee email and created your password to access the WM systems. This same login will be used to access VDI.

If you don't have your login credentials, please reach out to your direct supervisor.

Accessing VDI

Once you turn on your company-issued computer and access the desktop, the login page for VDI should pop up automatically. If it does not, click on the green computer icon (pictured below) to open a browser and navigate to the login page.

Once on this page, you will use your company-issued login credentials to enter the VDI system. After logging in, you will need to select the correct VDI interface. Reach out to your supervisor to confirm which one to select. You will then be prompted to enter your password again. Once completed, you will have successfully accessed the virtual desktop.

You may experience issues accessing the virtual environment on occasion. If an error occurs, it's important to try again a few times to see if the issue resolves itself. If you continue to receive errors, reach out to your supervisor for assistance.

KRONOS

KRONOS is our timekeeping application for tracking your work hours. It's important to know how to access this app to ensure you are paid for the hours you work. The icon to access KRONOS is pictured below.

You will access KRONOS at least four times a day:

  • At the start of your shift to clock in
  • When you clock out for your lunch break
  • When you clock back in from your lunch break
  • When you clock out at the end of the day

KRONOS is also used to request time off and view your accrued Holiday, Vacation, and Personal hours for time off requests.

Avaya Agent

Avaya Agent is the program we use to make and receive phone calls with customers. You'll need to access this program immediately after clocking in to ensure your phone system functions properly. The icon for this program is pictured below.

You'll need to log in to the Avaya Agent app in the Station section using your Station ID and a password. Your Station ID is essentially your phone number in the VDI system; however, you should not share it with anyone. You will also be given a password, which is the same for all agents.

If it's your first time accessing the Avaya Agent program on your computer, you will need to configure the settings. Reach out to your supervisor if you are unsure how to proceed or if you are prompted for additional information.

NICE

NICE is another app we use to manage our daily schedules. While KRONOS focuses on the overarching schedule, NICE provides more specific details about your daily tasks. NICE is accessed through the Google Chrome browser, and your supervisor should have given you the URL to log in. NICE is used for the following purposes:

  • Viewing your scheduled meetings
  • Checking your breaks and lunch times
  • Viewing your scheduled coaching sessions
  • Monitoring your call statistics, including average handle time and the number of calls taken
  • Reporting any downtime outside of your regular schedule, such as internet outages and system issues

NICE is updated regularly. While most days your schedule may remain the same, it's important to check it frequently for updates to ensure you remain in adherence.

DRIVE stands for Deliver Results, Increase Value, and Engagement. It is an extension of the NICE application mentioned earlier and serves as our internal hub for managing agent success.

DRIVE is essential for all employees, as it allows you to stay up to date on your performance trends and identify areas for improvement. Be sure to check DRIVE regularly and be prepared to discuss your progress with your supervisor during coaching sessions.

Twice a month, you will be required to attend coaching sessions with your supervisor. These one-on-one meetings will review your performance indicators for the current period and identify opportunities for improvement.

You will use DRIVE to conduct the coaching session, so be sure to check it before your scheduled session to prepare and note any topics you'd like to discuss. At the end of each session, your supervisor will document the results in DRIVE, acknowledging everything discussed and outlining any action plans based on the conversation.

Be sure that at the end of each coaching session, you go into DRIVE to acknowledge the session and leave any comments you may have.

Measuring Performance

Your Key Performance Indicators (KPIs) help supervisors assess your efficiency in handling calls. They provide insight into your performance, highlight areas for improvement, and determine whether you meet the company's success standards.

Below are the five key areas that will be periodically evaluated, as they have the greatest impact on your overall performance.

Quality

Quality refers to the overall effectiveness of your calls. Your supervisors will review a set number of calls each month and score them based on specific criteria. Your grade depends on meeting key elements of the call, including a professional greeting, demonstrating empathy, achieving first-call resolution, respecting the customerâ€™s time, and properly closing the call.

Average Handle Time

Average Handle Time refers to the average amount of time you spend on the phone with a customer. While delivering excellent service and addressing all concerns is essential, it is equally important to respect the customer's time and resolve issues efficiently.

Adherence

Adherence measures how accurately you follow your schedule. This includes clocking in and out on time, taking and returning from lunch breaks as scheduled, and being in the correct status. Schedules are designed to ensure agents are available to handle the workload, so it is essential to be where you are supposed to be, when you are supposed to be there.

After Call Waiting

After Call Waiting (ACW) refers to the average time you take to document and categorize calls after they end. After each call, you are required to leave notes and select the appropriate call category from the menu. If your ACW time is too high, it indicates that you are spending excessive time off the phones, which can impact overall efficiency.

PCS Survey Scores

Post Call Survey (PCS) scores are surveys randomly given to customers after their call with you. They provide insight into the customerâ€™s experience beyond other KPIs, measuring how the customer felt about you, how well you handled the call, and their confidence that their issue will be resolved.

Supervisors possess a wealth of knowledge and, like other agents, have likely encountered various situations, including more complex or niche issues. However, keep in mind that supervisors and team leads have their own workloads. Be sure to utilize all available resources to ensure efficiency and effectiveness in your role.

Tips for Success

At WM, we want you to succeed. After providing you with the necessary tools and resources, here are some valuable tips from experienced agents to further support your success.

  • Develop a call flow that aligns with all quality standards. Once you establish a rhythm that works, stick to it. Maintaining consistency will help you achieve consistently high-quality scores.
  • Use a note-taking app that you're comfortable with. Keep essential notes handy, such as frequently used phone numbers, email addresses, templates, and key talking points to improve efficiency.
  • Don't take it personally. You will likely encounter upset customers who may vent their frustrations to you. While you may not be directly responsible for the issue, you represent the company. Stay professional and remember that itâ€™s never personal.
  • Save bookmarks for quick access during calls. Organizing frequently visited resources will help you quickly retrieve important information, improving your average handle time and overall performance scores.

In the early stages, focus on familiarizing yourself with the programs, processes, and available resources. As you gain experience, you will develop your own workflow and become more confident in handling calls with ease.
